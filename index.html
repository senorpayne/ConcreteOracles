CONCRETE ORACLES


MATTHEW J. LAWLER

Concrete Oracles is a book of 41 poems about living in Chicago ("the vibrant city"). The author is a poet who came of age in an "ageless / aimless teenage kingdom," a poet who writes of drifters, outcasts, junkies, bums, Vietnam vets, and window washers, who mourns the loss of Eric Garner, Philando Castile, dead friends, and a young kid run down by a narcotics officer's Crown Vic, and who enshrines in poems people saved from fire and drowning, "mothers and children of diverse races," and even the tough Chicago streets he himself has walked. Matthew J. Lawler is the name of this poet who shunned gangs to fall "in love with dreams," a poet who writes of himself, "I, too, am finding."

Bill Yarrow, author of Against Prompts
